10. This reminds me of something from Taoism: "Be like water." Water doesn't push through rocks — it finds the path of least resistance. And it still gets there. What if we approached work the same way?

22. When did "being productive" become the only measure of a good day? What if the best days are the ones where you don't achieve anything but feel at peace?

### 补充型
24. There's a 2,500-year-old concept that solves this: Wu Wei. It means "action through non-action." Not being lazy — but being so aligned with what you're doing that effort disappears. Like an athlete in flow state. What if productivity isn't about doing more?

25. The Tao Te Ching says: "Nature does not hurry, yet everything is accomplished." Maybe the real productivity hack isn't a new app or morning routine — it's learning when to stop. Thoughts?
