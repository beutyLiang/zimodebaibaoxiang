The 3 Types of
Overthinking Traps

Trap 1: The Replay Loop

You keep replaying past decisions. "What if I chose differently?"

→ This drains energy on things you cannot change.

Trap 2: The Analysis Spiral

You research, compare, weigh options — but never choose.

→ More information doesn't create clarity. It creates confusion.

Trap 3: The Future Panic

You imagine worst-case scenarios that haven't happened.

→ You're solving problems that don't exist yet.

Which trap do you fall into most? Circle it. That's your starting point.

The Wu Wei Principle

Why Eastern Philosophy Works Here

Ancient Chinese philosophers discovered something 2,500 years ago:

The best decisions feel effortless. Not because they're easy — but because you stopped fighting yourself.

This is called Wu Wei (无为) — "effortless action."

It doesn't mean "do nothing." It means:

Stop forcing. Start flowing.

When you overthink, you're forcing your brain to find the "perfect" answer.

Wu Wei says: there is no perfect answer. There is only the next step.

The 7-Day Detox System

1
The Decision Audit

Write down every decision you made today. All of them. Now circle the ones that actually matter (hint: it's less than 5).

💡 Insight: 90% of your decisions don't deserve your energy.
Open your notes app and list every decision from the last 3 hours.
2
The 2-Minute Rule

For any decision that takes less than 2 minutes to reverse: decide NOW. Set a timer. When it rings, choose. No exceptions.

💡 Insight: Speed beats perfection for reversible decisions.
Pick one small decision you've been putting off — and decide in 2 minutes.
3
The "Good Enough" Practice

Pick one decision today and intentionally choose the "good enough" option. Not the best. Not the optimal. Just good enough.

💡 Insight: "Good enough" done today > "perfect" done never.
The next time you're choosing between options, pick the first one that's 70% right.

4
The Input Fast

No articles. No podcasts. No advice. For 24 hours. Your brain has enough information. It needs silence to process.

💡 Insight: More information ≠ better decisions.
Unfollow 3 accounts that make you overthink. Right now.
5
The Body Check

Before your next decision, close your eyes for 60 seconds. Ask: "What does my gut say?" Write it down. Then decide.

💡 Insight: Your body often knows the answer before your mind does.
Close your eyes. Think of a decision you're stuck on. What does your gut say? Write it down.
6
The Regret Reframe

Write down a past decision you regret. Now list 3 things that happened BECAUSE of that decision — things that wouldn't have happened otherwise.

💡 Insight: Every "wrong" decision created something right.
Write down your biggest regret. Now find 3 good things that came from it.
7
The Next Step Protocol

Stop asking: "What's the best choice?" Start asking: "What's the smallest next step?"

💡 Insight: Clarity comes from action, not from thinking.
What's the ONE thing you've been overthinking? Write down the smallest possible next step. Do it.

Reduce → Align → Act

The Core Framework

This is the system behind everything in this guide. Memorize it. Use it daily.

R

Reduce

Cut the noise. Fewer inputs, fewer options, fewer criteria. When in doubt, remove — don't add.

A

Align

Check with your gut, not just your head. If it doesn't feel right, it isn't — no matter how logical it looks.

A

Act

Take the smallest next step. Now. Don't wait for certainty — certainty comes after action, not before.

10 minutes. That's all it takes. Reduce the noise. Align with your gut. Act on the smallest step. Done.
