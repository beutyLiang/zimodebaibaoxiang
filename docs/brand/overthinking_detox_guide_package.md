### Cover

*A 7-Day System to Stop Mental Spirals and Make Clear Decisions*

### Page 1 — Your Brain Is Not the Problem

You make 35,000 decisions every day.

No wonder you're exhausted before noon.

But here's what nobody tells you:
**Overthinking is not a personality flaw. It's a signal.**

Your mind is trying to protect you — from mistakes, from regret, from the unknown.

The problem? **Protection mode became your default mode.**

This guide will show you how to stop it — **today.** Not with willpower. With a system.

### Page 2 — The 3 Types of Overthinking Traps

**Trap 1: The Replay Loop**
You keep replaying past decisions. "What if I chose differently?"
→ This drains energy on things you cannot change.

**Trap 2: The Analysis Spiral**
You research, compare, weigh options — but never choose.
→ More information doesn't create clarity. It creates confusion.

**Trap 3: The Future Panic**
You imagine worst-case scenarios that haven't happened.
→ You're solving problems that don't exist yet.

*Which trap do you fall into most? Circle it. That's your starting point.*

### Page 3 — The Wu Wei Principle (Why Eastern Philosophy Works Here)

Ancient Chinese philosophers discovered something 2,500 years ago:

> **The best decisions feel effortless. Not because they're easy — but because you stopped fighting yourself.**

This is called Wu Wei (无为) — "effortless action."

It doesn't mean "do nothing." It means:
**Stop forcing. Start flowing.**

When you overthink, you're forcing your brain to find the "perfect" answer.
Wu Wei says: **there is no perfect answer. There is only the next step.**

### Page 4-5 — The 7-Day Detox System

**Day 1: The Decision Audit**
Write down every decision you made today. All of them.
Now circle the ones that actually matter (hint: it's less than 5).
→ *Insight: 90% of your decisions don't deserve your energy.*
**Do this immediately:** Open your notes app and list every decision from the last 3 hours.

**Day 2: The 2-Minute Rule**
For any decision that takes less than 2 minutes to reverse: decide NOW.
Set a timer. When it rings, choose. No exceptions.
→ *Insight: Speed beats perfection for reversible decisions.*
**Do this now:** Pick one small decision you've been putting off — and decide in 2 minutes.

**Day 3: The "Good Enough" Practice**
Pick one decision today and intentionally choose the "good enough" option.
Not the best. Not the optimal. Just good enough.
→ *Insight: "Good enough" done today > "perfect" done never.*
**Do this immediately:** The next time you're choosing between options, pick the first one that's 70% right.

**Day 4: The Input Fast**
No articles. No podcasts. No advice. For 24 hours.
Your brain has enough information. It needs silence to process.
→ *Insight: More information ≠ better decisions.*
**Do this immediately:** Unfollow 3 accounts that make you overthink. Right now.

**Day 5: The Body Check**
Before your next decision, close your eyes for 60 seconds.
Ask: "What does my gut say?" Write it down. Then decide.
→ *Insight: Your body often knows the answer before your mind does.*
**Do this now:** Close your eyes. Think of a decision you're stuck on. What does your gut say? Write it down.

**Day 6: The Regret Reframe**
Write down a past decision you regret. Now list 3 things that happened BECAUSE of that decision — things that wouldn't have happened otherwise.
→ *Insight: Every "wrong" decision created something right.*
**Do this immediately:** Write down your biggest regret. Now find 3 good things that came from it.

**Day 7: The Next Step Protocol**
Stop asking: "What's the best choice?"
Start asking: "What's the smallest next step?"
→ *Insight: Clarity comes from action, not from thinking.*
**Do this now:** What's the ONE thing you've been overthinking? Write down the smallest possible next step. Do it.

### Page 6 — The 60-Second Reset (Use This When You're Stuck)

**This is the only thing you need to remember.**

When overthinking hits, do this in 60 seconds:

1. Name the trap (Replay / Analysis / Future Panic)
2. Ask: "Is this decision reversible?" (90% are)
3. Set a 2-minute timer
4. Choose. Move. Don't look back.

**Print this page. Tape it to your monitor. Use it every time.**

### Page 7 — The Reduce → Align → Act Framework

This is the core system behind everything in this guide:

**Reduce** — Cut the noise. Fewer inputs, fewer options, fewer criteria.
**Align** — Check with your gut, not just your head.
**Act** — Take the smallest next step. Now.

10 minutes. That's all it takes.
